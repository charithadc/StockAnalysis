01 — Overview

Executive Summary

LPL Financial is the nation's largest independent broker-dealer, having more than tripled its advisor base and assets under management in the past five years through a relentless acquisition and organic-growth strategy. As of April 2026, it manages $2.4 trillion in advisory and brokerage assets across 32,000+ advisors — and is still early in the operating leverage cycle.

This report forecasts Revenue per Share, EPS, and P/E ranges for 2026–2029, anchored in full-year 2025 actuals, analyst consensus, and LPL's structural tailwinds from advisor recruitment, Commonwealth integration, and its expanding advisory-fee mix.

Note: LPL Financial's fiscal year follows the calendar year (January–December). All EPS figures are Adjusted (non-GAAP) unless otherwise stated. GAAP EPS is materially lower due to acquisition-related amortisation.

03 — Business

Three Revenue Pillars

  • Advisory Fees (~51% of gross revenue) — Asset-based fees on $1.4T in advisory assets (58.8% of total). Advisory assets grew 46% YoY in 2025. This is the fastest-growing and highest-quality revenue stream — recurring, fee-based, and tied to market appreciation and net new assets.
  • Commission Revenue (~31%) — Sales-based and trailing commissions from brokerage accounts. Grew 36% in 2025 driven by acquisitions. More transactional, but large scale provides defensibility.
  • Asset-Based & Other (~18%) — Client cash sweep revenue ($50.6B in sweep balances as of Q4 2025), other asset-based fees, service and transaction fees, and interest income. Cash sweep income is rate-sensitive; current yield environment is supportive.

LPL's revenue mix is actively shifting toward recurring advisory fees — 58.8% of assets are now advisory (up from 55.0% a year ago) — which structurally raises earnings quality, operating leverage, and deserved P/E multiples.

Why P/E May Re-Rate Upward

  • Bear case (13–14x Adj. EPS): Equity market correction shrinks AUM; rate cuts compress cash sweep income; macro slowdown causes advisor fee attrition. EPS stalls near $21–22. Stock range $280–310.
  • Base case (15–16x Adj. EPS): Integration delivers on plan. Steady 10–13% EPS growth. P/E stays near current levels. Most likely outcome per consensus.
  • Bull case (17–18x Adj. EPS): Achievable if Commonwealth beats synergy targets, advisory mix accelerates past 65%, and/or another institutional acquisition adds accretion. Analyst high target of $504 implies roughly 17–18x forward Adj. EPS.

06 — Catalysts

Key Bull Case Drivers

  • Commonwealth integration synergies: The $2.7B acquisition (closed August 2025) added ~3,000 advisors and ~$300B in assets. Management projected 90% asset retention. Synergies on technology consolidation and G&A leverage are still largely ahead.
  • Organic growth flywheel: LPL added $147B in net new assets in 2025 — 8% organic growth. Advisor recruiting remains the industry's strongest pipeline, supported by LPL's technology platform, custodial capabilities, and no proprietary product conflicts.
  • Advisory mix shift: As brokerage assets convert to advisory, revenue becomes more predictable and margins expand. Advisory assets already 58.8% of total AUM, up from 55% a year ago — each percentage point of conversion adds recurring fee revenue without incremental cost.
  • Cash sweep tailwind: LPL holds $50.6B in client cash sweeps. With the Fed likely to cut rates gradually rather than aggressively, sweep income remains a significant earnings contributor through the forecast period.
  • Capital return programme: Regular quarterly dividends ($0.30/share) plus opportunistic buybacks. Share count is broadly stable (~79–80M diluted), providing modest EPS lift as any shrinkage compounds over time.
  • Valuation discount to ROE: LPL's ROE of 37.9% (5-yr average) — forecast to reach 53%+ in 3 years — would typically command a premium multiple. At 14–15x Adj. EPS, the market is ascribing a discount that may correct as integration costs recede and earnings quality improves.

07 — Risks

Key Risk Factors

HIGH
Equity Market Correction LPL earns advisory fees as a percentage of AUM. A sustained 20–30% equity market decline would mechanically reduce advisory revenue by a similar amount, compressing EPS well below forecasts. Beta of 0.57 limits stock volatility but doesn't remove earnings exposure.
HIGH
Interest Rate Risk (Cash Sweep Compression) Aggressive Fed rate cuts would directly reduce net interest income from $50.6B in sweep balances. A 100bps cut could reduce pre-tax income by ~$300–400M, a meaningful drag on EPS. This is the most rate-sensitive line item in the P&L.
MEDIUM
Integration Execution Risk Commonwealth, Atria, and Investment Center collectively represent the largest integration challenge in LPL's history. Advisor attrition above the 10% modelled rate, technology migration failures, or G&A overruns could erode the earnings thesis materially.
MEDIUM
Fee Compression & Regulatory Risk Ongoing regulatory scrutiny of advisor compensation, Reg BI compliance costs, and potential fee compression across the industry could narrow advisory margins. DOL fiduciary rule evolution adds ongoing compliance overhead.
LOW
Debt Load from Acquisitions Total debt reached $7.3B at year-end 2025 (up from $5.5B prior year). While manageable given $2.9B in Adjusted EBITDA, a sharp earnings slowdown could strain leverage ratios and limit future M&A optionality.
LOW
Advisor Competitive Attrition Raymond James, Ameriprise, and RIA aggregators are all competing for the same advisor pool. While LPL's platform and technology lead is substantial, premium recruiting packages from rivals could slow net new advisor growth.

Overall assessment: LPL carries moderate market and rate risk but low idiosyncratic business risk. Its position as the dominant independent BD platform, diversified revenue across 32,000+ advisors, and exceptional ROE make it resilient. Primary downside risk is macro and rate-driven rather than competitive or operational.

08 — Conclusion

Investment Conclusion

LPL Financial is the undisputed platform of record for independent wealth management in the United States. Its scale — 32,000 advisors, $2.4 trillion in assets — creates a structural competitive moat that is widening, not narrowing. The stock is currently priced at ~14–15x forward Adjusted EPS, a meaningful discount to its earnings quality and ROE profile.
