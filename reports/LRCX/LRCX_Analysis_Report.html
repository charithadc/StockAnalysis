01 — Overview

Executive Summary

Lam Research is one of the world's most critical semiconductor equipment makers — supplying the etch, deposition, and clean tools essential to every advanced chip fabricated on the planet. As of April 2026, the company just posted record Q3 FY2026 revenue of $5.84 billion and guided to $6.60 billion for Q4, with AI-driven wafer fab equipment (WFE) spending projected to reach $140 billion in CY2026 — a major demand tailwind.

This report forecasts Revenue per Share, EPS, and P/E ranges for FY2026–FY2029, anchored in the latest earnings results, management guidance, analyst consensus, and Lam's structural positioning across memory, foundry, and advanced packaging.

03 — Business

Revenue Streams & Markets Served

  • Systems Revenue (~60% of total) — New etch, deposition, and clean capital equipment sold to chipmakers. Foundry accounted for 54% of systems revenue in Q3 FY26 (up 35% YoY), fuelled by leading-edge logic and AI accelerator fab expansion at TSMC and Samsung.
  • Customer Support Business Group (~40%) — Spares, upgrades, services, and Reliant (non-leading-edge) tools. Hit $2.1 billion in Q3 FY26 — its first $2B+ quarter — growing 25% YoY and providing highly predictable, high-margin recurring revenue.
  • Memory Segment (~39% of systems) — DRAM and NAND. DRAM surged 36% QoQ in Q2 FY26 driven by HBM (High Bandwidth Memory) for AI. NAND is in early recovery, with Lam uniquely exposed to NAND tech transitions through its etch and deposition dominance.

Lam's customer mix spans the globe: China 34%, Korea 23%, Taiwan 23% (Q3 FY26). Geographic diversification is both a strength and a risk — particularly given ongoing US export control restrictions targeting China's advanced chip sector.

P/E Re-Rating Scenarios

  • Bear case (22–28x): WFE cycle peaks earlier than expected, China revenue curtailed further by export controls, NAND oversupply returns, or macro recession cuts enterprise chip demand.
  • Base case (28–36x): Steady WFE growth of 10–15% annually, HBM and GAA transitions sustain etch intensity, operating margins hold 34–37%. Most likely outcome given current trajectory.
  • Bull case (38–48x): AI chip demand meaningfully exceeds expectations, WFE surpasses $160B by 2028, NAND recovery faster than anticipated, and Lam gains share in advanced logic etch. P/E could sustain elevated levels akin to current pricing.

06 — Catalysts

Key Bull Case Drivers

  • AI-driven WFE supercycle: WFE spending is now forecast at $140B for CY2026 — up from $135B prior guidance — and biased to the upside. Every AI chip fab buildout requires Lam's etch and deposition tools at the critical patterning and memory formation steps.
  • HBM memory intensity: High-Bandwidth Memory for AI accelerators requires significantly more etch steps per wafer than conventional DRAM, boosting Lam's revenue per wafer start. DRAM grew 36% QoQ in Q2 FY26 as HBM demand accelerated.
  • Foundry capex expansion: TSMC, Samsung, and Intel Foundry are all aggressively expanding. Foundry represented 54% of systems revenue in Q3 FY26 — its highest share in years — as GAA transistor transitions require new etch and deposition tools.
  • Recurring services flywheel: The Customer Support Business Group hit a milestone $2.1B quarter. As Lam's installed base compounds, spares and upgrade revenue grows passively and predictably — a structural margin tailwind.
  • Aggressive capital return: Lam spent $3.4B on buybacks in FY2025 alone, shrinking the share count. With $4.8B in cash and strong free cash flow generation, this programme is set to continue — mechanically boosting EPS above revenue growth.

07 — Risks

Key Risk Factors

HIGH
China Export Control Escalation China represented 34% of Q3 FY26 revenue. Any tightening of US export rules — restricting advanced etch or deposition tools — could materially impact near-term revenues and is the single largest overhang on the stock.
HIGH
WFE Cycle Downturn Semiconductor equipment spending is inherently cyclical. A memory oversupply cycle (particularly NAND) or a pause in foundry capex could result in sharp order cancellations and revenue declines, as seen in FY2023's down-cycle.
MEDIUM
Competitive Pressure from Tokyo Electron & Applied Materials AMAT and TEL compete directly in etch and deposition. Customers qualifying multiple suppliers creates pricing pressure. Loss of a key tool qualification at a major customer would be a significant revenue headwind.
MEDIUM
Macro / Tariff Shock A global recession or escalating tariff regime between the US and key trading partners (South Korea, Taiwan, China) could freeze capex decisions and delay equipment orders across Lam's customer base.
LOW
Supply Chain & Execution Risk Complex tool manufacturing requires sourcing specialised components. Disruptions could create delivery delays. However, Lam's $2.22B deferred revenue balance indicates strong backlog and production visibility.

Overall assessment: LRCX carries moderate cyclical risk inherent to the semiconductor equipment industry. China concentration is the primary near-term concern. However, the multi-year AI and memory upgrade cycle provides a durable structural offset that makes this a fundamentally compelling business through at least FY2029.
